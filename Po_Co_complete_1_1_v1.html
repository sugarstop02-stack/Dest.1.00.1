What Is Polyscript?

Polyscript Glyph Arrays are a living alphabet. Every word is built from four parts stacked together:

★ ROOTS — the shape (consonants) — what the word IS
★ TONES — the feeling (vowels) — how the word FEELS
★ SHIFTS — the direction (Day/Night) — which way the word FACES
★ MODES — the operation (Hive/Leach) — what the word DOES
Two Ways to Write
Archive Script

Pictographic glyphs. Stack them vertically: Root → Tone → Shift → Mode.

Glyph Arrays

Compass drawing. Map sounds to 8 directions, draw lines, connect them.

Both are real Polyscript. Both are you.

Lesson 00 What Is a Glyph?

Every Polyscript word is a glyph. A glyph is not just a letter — it is an entire meaning-structure built from four layers stacked from top to bottom.

# Layer Archive Name What It Does Question It Answers
1 Root The God (Consonant) Gives the glyph its shape What IS this thing?
2 Tone The Race (Vowel) Gives the glyph its feeling How does it FEEL?
3 Shift The Direction Outward (Day) or Inward (Night) Which way does it FACE?
4 Mode The Operation HIVE (gives) or LEACH (holds) What does it DO?

Layer 4 — Modes Leach & Hive — What Words Do

Every glyph operates in one of two Modes. The Mode tells you what the glyph does — does it release, or does it hold?

⊥
Hive Mode
Sound: HI  ·  Sun Mode
Releases. Gives. Reveals. Acts. The Hive removes what doesn't need to be held and gives it outward to the world.
Release · Give · Change · Act
⊣
Leach Mode
Sound: LE  ·  Moon Mode
Absorbs. Holds. Accepts. Remembers. The Leach takes in what it receives and keeps it close — for later, for always.
Absorb · Hold · Accept · Remember
Neither Is Better. Both Are Sacred.

The Archivist who knows when to hold and when to release holds great power. Some things are worth keeping. Some things are worth setting down.

"The Archivist who knows when to hold and when to release holds great power." — The Archive

Archive Lesson · Language Structure The Four Scales of Language

Every language — in your world and in the Archive — organizes sound into layers. Polyscript has four scales. Each one is built from the one below it. Together, they carry everything language can say.

1
Rune
= Letter
One Sound · One Mark
The smallest unit. A single phonetic symbol — one mouth movement, one mark on the page. Runes are the atoms of Polyscript.
Example
CO  ·  LE  ·  B  ·  EE
↓
2
Glyph
= Word
Root · Tone · Shift · Mode
A complete Archive word. Four rune-layers stacked into one meaning. Every glyph has a shape, a feeling, a direction, and an operation.
Example
COLEBEE  → Day · LE
↓
3
Tarot
= Sentence
Glyphs in Relationship
A Tarot is a complete thought — glyphs arranged so they act on each other. A Tarot has a subject, a motion, and a result, just like a sentence.
Example
COLEBEE · Day - - - LE
↓
4
Oracle
= Paragraph
Tarots in Motion
An Oracle is a sustained Archive transmission — multiple Tarots unified by one tone or purpose. It is the largest natural unit of Polyscript. It tells the whole story.
Structure
16 Tarots max
