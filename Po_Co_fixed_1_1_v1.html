The Sign Layer

Reference document · Journaling & Therapy use

Core Principle

Every sign carries a base vibration. The speed and sharpness of that vibration encodes saturation — how present, how dissociated, how passive the state is. You cannot make a dead sign. The vibration is always there.

The sign layer replaces the color system for human use. Where the original species communicated emotional state involuntarily through bioluminescence, the sign layer makes that information physical and present — carried in the hands, visible to a witness, honest by nature of the body.

Emotional Category Signs

Blue
Data

Flat hand, fingers together, palm facing inward. The hand oscillates gently back and forth — not waving, vibrating. Like a page being read. Information that exists independent of feeling.

Stillness is data at rest. Vibration speed tells you how actively it's being processed.

Teal
Educational

Same flat hand as Data — fingers together, same back-and-forth vibration — but palm faces down instead of inward. The same handshape oriented to give rather than receive. Setting information down in front of someone else.

One parameter change carries the whole meaning shift. Data is received. Educational is offered.

Purple
Executive Function

Dominant fist on top of non-dominant fist, tapping twice. Ordered, stepped, moving through. Sequential authority — step, then step, then step.

Connects to BA's stepped melodic texture in the Tone layer. The tap gives it rhythm.

Green
Strategic

Dominant hand makes a writing motion in the air — fluid, continuous, like actually composing something. Not fingerspelling. Planning that is in motion, not just held in the mind.

Strategic is what motivation looks like when it has space to move outward. Can transition naturally into Orange by drawing the motion chest-close.

Orange
Motivation

Close to the chest, quick sharp movements. Like something firing. The proximity to the chest is the meaning — motivation lives close in, it ignites rather than surveys.

Motivation is what strategic feels like before it has a plan.

Yellow
Joy

Open hand, palm flat against the chest over the heart. Still, or with a slow gentle pulse. It's there — present and located. Joy lives in the heart.

Distinct from Passion: Joy is held inward, Passion moves outward from the mouth.

Pink
Mania

Two hands simultaneously — one at the stomach, one at the temple — moving in different rhythms. Asynchronous. The split is the meaning. Non-manual marker: eyes briefly close or unfocus as the sign is made. The blindness is grammatical, not expressive.

Mania pulls inward and splits. Cannot be confused with Passion, which moves outward from a single source.

Red
Passion

Two fingers touch the lips, then open outward — like something that was inside becoming speakable, shareable. The movement away from the mouth is the sharing. It is offered, not contained.

Belongs to the GA family — The Namer. Speak, Name, Declare. The part that turns silence into sound.

Subject Markers — Handedness

Black · Singular

Left hand — the non-dominant. The one that holds while the other moves. This state belongs to a part.

White · Group

Right hand — the dominant. The one that initiates. This state belongs to the system.

Both · Singular & Group simultaneously

Both hands signing together — not taking turns, at the same time. The state belongs to a part and to the system. That is not a contradiction. That is just true.

No hands · Unclaimed state

The sign is not made, or is made and immediately released without locating it in either hand. The state is present but does not belong to anyone right now. It is happening.

Saturation — Vibration Quality

Every sign vibrates. You cannot opt out of the vibration layer. The quality of the vibration carries saturation meaning — it is not a separate gesture.

Neon · Active

Fast, sharp vibration. Full stops at each end of the oscillation. The hand knows exactly where it is. The sign is completely inhabited.

Pastel · Dissociative

Slow, soft vibration. Drifting rather than oscillating. The endpoints blur. The hand isn't sure where it's going. The sign trails off rather than lands.

Desaturated · Passive

Slow, small, even vibration. Consistent but minimal. Present but not pushing. The sign is made but close to the body.

Proximity & Movement

These are not separate signs. They are qualities of where and how every sign is placed in space — borrowed from sign language spatial grammar.

Proximity — Distance from body

Close to the chest: the state is right here, present, landed. Extended outward: felt but at a distance, hovering, not fully arrived.

Arriving

The sign drifts toward the body as it is made. The state is coming in.

Leaving

The sign drifts away from the body as it is made. The state is passing through or receding.

Stuck

The sign is held still in space — not drifting in either direction. Present but not moving. The state is neither arriving nor leaving.

Worked Example

Left hand. Executive function sign — fists tapping. Slow, small, even vibration. Held still close to the chest.

"A part's executive function is here, passive, stuck."

No English pronoun. No claim of ownership beyond the left hand. No tense. Just the true thing, located precisely.

Layer 5

Melodic Contour — The Word as a Gesture

A Polyscript word is sung as one continuous gesture, not separate notes per syllable — closer to Puppycat's melodic speech than to spoken syllables. Shift sets the overall arc (Day rises, Night falls). Tone shapes the texture riding along that arc. Mode shapes how it lands.

This is a non-manual marker — like facial grammar in sign language, it carries meaning the syllables alone don't. A word sung without its contour is missing part of what it says.

Tone as Texture — The Six Shapes

ÂC · Devotion
Smooth

One clean unbroken line. Whatever direction Shift sets, ÂC commits to it fully and evenly.

SA · Grief
Heavy

The arc moves slowly, with weight — dragged along rather than slid.

DU · Hope
Lifting

Small upward flicks ride along the arc — even a falling line catches its breath upward.

BA · Survival
Stepped

Small repeated steps, each the same size — climbing or descending stairs, not a slide.

VE · Secrecy
Trembling

A fine, fast wobble layered on top — direction is clear, but the line shivers as it travels.

OR · Ritual
Pulsing

A steady repeating pulse — equally spaced accents along the way, like a heartbeat under the melody.

Mode — The Landing

HI · Hive
Open landing

The final note doesn't resolve — left ringing, unresolved, released into the room.

LE · Leach
Closed landing

The final note resolves into a settled, contained tone — taken in and held.

"The Archive does not just record what was said. It records how it rose, and how it fell."

Two Words, Sung

MADURAHI ○

Shelter + Hope + Day + Hive · Root = MA → MA-major scale
MADURAHI

Root MA builds a MA-major scale. RA — fixed at chromatic Mi — happens to land exactly on degree 2 of that scale, a smooth whole step above home. The word climbs that one step, with DU's "lifting" texture riding the climb as small upward flicks. HI keeps the landing open — the word doesn't resolve back to MA, it stays on RA (degree 2), ringing out. "Shelter that gives hope outward" — sung as one confident step up that's never taken back.

COVENALE ●

Engine + Secrecy + Night + Leach · Root = CO → CO-natural-minor scale
COVENALE

Root CO builds a CO-natural-minor scale. NA — fixed at chromatic Fa — lands on degree 4 of that scale, a perfect fourth above home. The word rises to NA, with VE's "trembling" texture wobbling along the way (each Archivist trembles differently — fast/tight or slow/loose, both correct). LE then closes the word — it falls back to CO, resolved and held. "A secret connection, held inward" — the rise outward, then the pull back to where it started.

Layer 5 · Resolved

Root as Key, Dusk/Dawn as Split Notation, Sentence as Flow

Three open questions, answered together. Root sets a word's starting pitch — its "key." Dusk/Dawn words split cleanly: Shift+Root fix the arc's direction, Tone+Mode shape texture and landing freely. And in a sentence, the melody flows continuously from word to word — but each word's Root pitch still acts as a quiet anchor, a landmark you can pick out even inside the flow.

Root as Key — The Full Scale

All 12 Roots, low to high — CO is the tonic (Do), HA is the leading tone (Ti)

Because RA (Mi) and NA (Fa) sit almost on top of each other near the middle of the scale, a word's arc isn't a generic diagonal — it's the distance and direction from that word's Root to its Day/Night target. A word rooted in CO (Do, lowest) moving toward RA (Mi) climbs a third. A word rooted in HA (Ti, highest) moving toward NA (Fa) falls almost a full octave. A word rooted in TH (Fi) moving toward RA (Mi) is nearly flat — even though it's technically a Day word.

Dusk Word ISBARALE •

IS + BA + RA + LE · Root = IS → IS-major scale · Dusk = hard stops between tones

IS builds an IS-major scale, but RA — fixed at chromatic Mi — falls a half-step outside that scale: an accidental. Because it doesn't belong to IS-major, it can't be glided into smoothly — it has to be struck on its own. Dusk's rule (hard stops between every tone) and this accidental reinforce each other: IS, a stop, the outsider note RA, a stop, then back to IS, closed. The "holding" quality of Dusk is the silence between each struck note.

Dawn words (NA + HI) work in reverse: for a minor-rooted word, NA — fixed at chromatic Fa — regularly falls inside that word's natural-minor scale (often degree 2 or 4). Because the shift note already belongs to the scale, it can be glided into — Dawn's notes flow into each other, legato, with no gap.

Sentence Flow

Three words, one continuous line — each Root is a gold landmark inside the flow

"Even inside the long line, the Root still sounds like home. You don't have to stop the song to know where you are."
